# Financial Guide for Bootstrapping Founders

## 1. Financial Planning Before Quitting

### Runway Calculation
Before leaving a salaried job, calculate your "Runway" - the number of months you can survive without a salary.
- **Formula**: `Runway = (Liquid Savings - Emergency Fund) / Monthly Burn Rate`
- **Burn Rate**: Calculate two versions:
    1.  **Comfort Budget**: Current lifestyle maintained.
    2.  **Austerity Budget**: Bare minimum expenses (rent/mortgage, food, utilities, insurance).
- **Recommendation**: Aim for 12-18 months of *Austerity* runway or 9-12 months of *Comfort* runway before quitting.

### Emergency Fund
- **Distinct from Runway**: Do not count your emergency fund as part of your business runway.
- **Purpose**: For unforeseen personal disasters (medical emergency, car repair) that are unrelated to the business.
- **Size**: 6 months of expenses is standard, but 12 months is safer for founders due to income volatility.

## 2. Managing Personal Finances During Bootstrapping

### Cutting Costs
- **Big Wins**: Housing (move to cheaper area/co-living), Transport (sell second car), Subscriptions.
- **Negotiation**: Call service providers (internet, insurance) to negotiate lower rates.

### Health Insurance
- **Critical**: Do not go without health insurance. One medical event can wipe out your runway.
- **Options**:
    - Spouse's plan (often cheapest).
    - COBRA (expensive but seamless).
    - Marketplace/Exchange plans (subsidies may apply if income is low).
    - Health sharing ministries (cheaper, but not true insurance - risky).

### Retirement Contributions
- **Pause vs. Continue**: It is common to pause contributions during the pre-revenue phase to maximize liquidity.
- **Compound Interest**: Be aware of the long-term cost of pausing. Resume as soon as revenue allows.

## 3. Common Pitfalls

### Co-mingling Funds
- **Mistake**: Using personal credit cards for business expenses without tracking.
- **Fix**: Open a separate business checking account and credit card immediately. Treat transfers as "Owner's Equity" or "Shareholder Loans".

### Lifestyle Creep
- **Mistake**: Maintaining a "salaried" lifestyle while earning $0.
- **Fix**: Adopt the "student mindset" again.

### Underestimating Time-to-Revenue
- **Mistake**: Assuming revenue will start in Month 3.
- **Reality**: It often takes 2x-3x longer than expected to get the first paying customer.

## 4. Strategies for Extending Runway

### Fractional Work / Consulting
- **Strategy**: Work 1-2 days a week as a consultant in your domain.
- **Benefit**: Covers basic expenses, extending runway indefinitely.
- **Risk**: Distraction from the core product. Set strict boundaries (e.g., "Consulting Tuesdays").

### Pre-sales
- **Strategy**: Sell the product before building it.
- **Benefit**: Validates demand and provides immediate cash flow.

### Grants and Credits
- **Strategy**: Apply for startup credits (AWS, Google Cloud, Stripe) to reduce business burn.
