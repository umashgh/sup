# Financial Aspects of Leaving a Salaried Job to Bootstrap a Venture

## 1. Financial Planning Before Quitting

### Runway Calculation
The "Runway" is the duration you can survive without a salary.
*   **Formula**: `(Total Liquid Savings - Emergency Fund) / Monthly Burn Rate`
*   **Monthly Burn Rate**: The minimum amount needed to cover essential living expenses (Housing, Food, Utilities, Insurance, Debt obligations).
*   **Recommendation**: Calculate based on "Austerity Budget" (bare minimum) vs "Comfort Budget" (sustainable lifestyle).

### Emergency Fund
*   **Distinct from Runway**: This fund is for unforeseen personal disasters (medical emergency, car repair, family crisis), *not* for daily living or business expenses.
*   **Size**: 6-12 months of living expenses is recommended for founders due to the high volatility of their income.

### Debt Management
*   **High-Interest Debt**: Should be eliminated before quitting (Credit cards, personal loans).
*   **Low-Interest Debt**: (e.g., Mortgage) Ensure payments are factored into the burn rate. Refinancing to lower payments before leaving the salaried job can be a strategy.

## 2. Managing Personal Finances During Bootstrapping

### Cost Cutting ("Ramen Profitability" for the Home)
*   **Audit**: Review last 12 months of expenses.
*   **Cuts**: Eliminate discretionary spending (subscriptions, dining out, expensive vacations).
*   **Downsizing**: Moving to a cheaper apartment or city (geo-arbitrage) is a common high-impact strategy.

### Health Insurance & Benefits
*   **Coverage Gap**: Leaving a job means losing employer-sponsored health insurance.
*   **Action**: Secure a private health insurance policy *before* the last day. In India, ensure coverage includes all dependents and critical illness riders.
*   **Life Insurance**: Term insurance is critical if there are dependents, as the employer's group cover will cease.

### Retirement Contributions
*   **The Trade-off**: Pausing retirement contributions (PF/NPS/401k) extends runway but hurts long-term compounding.
*   **Strategy**: Many founders pause contributions during the "Valley of Death" (first 12-18 months) but should have a plan to resume once the business generates cash flow.

## 3. Common Financial Pitfalls

### Co-mingling Funds
*   **The Error**: Using the same bank account for business and personal expenses.
*   **The Fix**: Strict separation. Open a business current account immediately. Pay yourself a "salary" (even if irregular) rather than treating the business account as a personal ATM.

### Underestimating the "Time to Revenue"
*   **The Reality**: It almost always takes longer than expected to get the first paying customer.
*   **The Risk**: Running out of personal runway just as the product is ready.

### Lifestyle Creep with Early Revenue
*   **The Error**: Increasing personal spending as soon as the first check comes in.
*   **The Fix**: Reinvest early revenue into the business or extend the runway; keep personal burn low until Product-Market Fit (PMF) is established.

## 4. Benchmarks for Runway

*   **Minimum**: 6 months (High risk, high stress). Only advisable if you have a validated MVP and a clear path to immediate revenue.
*   **Recommended**: 12-18 months. This allows time for pivoting, product development, and sales cycles without panic.
*   **Ideal**: 24 months+. Allows for "deep work" and weathering economic downturns.

## 5. Strategies for Extending Runway

### Fractional Work / Consulting
*   **Strategy**: Dedicate 1-2 days a week to high-paid consulting in your domain of expertise.
*   **Benefit**: Covers living expenses without dipping into savings, effectively making runway infinite.
*   **Risk**: Distraction from the core startup mission.

### Pre-sales and Deposits
*   **Strategy**: Sell the product before it's built.
*   **Benefit**: Validates demand and provides non-dilutive capital.

### Leveraging Non-Cash Resources
*   **Credits**: Utilize startup credits for hosting (AWS/Google Cloud), software (Stripe, HubSpot), and tools.
*   **Grants**: Look for government grants or non-equity accelerator programs.
